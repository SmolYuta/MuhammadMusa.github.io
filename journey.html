MY STORY

Journey & Struggles

My path from beginner to a more confident student developer.

MY JOURNEY

How I Grew in Coding

Beginning

I first became interested in coding when I started learning how websites and games are made. It felt exciting because I was discovering a new world of technology and creativity.

Curiosity Grew

As I learned more, I started enjoying the idea that coding can turn imagination into something real. A few lines of code could become a page, a design, or even part of a game.

Practice Stage

I kept practicing and slowly became more familiar with structure, styling, layout, and interactions. Each small success made me feel more motivated to continue.

Project Building

After learning the basics, I started building actual projects. This was the stage where coding felt more meaningful, because I could see my ideas appear on the screen.

Today

Now I can create websites, project pages, and game-related work with more confidence. I know I still have more to learn, but I am proud of my improvement.

THE HARD PARTS

Struggles I Faced

Feeling Confused

In the beginning, many coding lessons felt confusing. New tags, rules, and functions seemed difficult, and sometimes I could not understand what each line was doing.

Small Errors, Big Problems

Sometimes one tiny mistake, like a missing bracket or a spelling error, could stop the whole project from working.

Slow Confidence

There were times when I felt other students were faster than me. That sometimes made me doubt myself and feel behind.

Not Giving Up

Even when learning felt hard, I kept trying. Every struggle taught me patience, courage, and the importance of practicing again and again.

WHAT I LEARNED

What This Journey Taught Me

Patience

I learned to stay calm and fix problems step by step instead of giving up.

Creativity

I learned that coding is not only about rules. It is also about creating something new and exciting.

Confidence

Each project helped me believe more in myself and in my ability to improve.
